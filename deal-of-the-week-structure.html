The Framework

The Deal of the Week Structure

The email framework built by Sharran Srivatsaa. Every email Deal of the Week Pro delivers follows this structure exactly: what goes in, what stays out, and why it earns replies.

Why It Works

One curated deal, every week, written like a note from a friend who knows the market.

Positioning

You become the insider

A weekly deal-curation email positions you as the person who sees the market first. Not a blast. A pick.

The Goal

Replies, not clicks

The email is built to start conversations, not drive showings or link clicks. The reply is the win. Everything else follows from a conversation.

The Compounding

Consistency is the asset

Week after week, your list learns to expect you. That is a lifetime nurture asset, and it compounds.

The Anatomy

Five parts, in order. Every part has one job.

1

Subject line

The format is locked:

Deal of the week in [City]: [one concrete specific]

The specific is a real number, a real feature, or an off-market hook. Never filler. "Deal of the week in Irvine: 4-bed off-market at $835K" earns the open. "Deal of the week!" does not.

2

Line 1

A first-person hook only you could write: a personal rule, a take, a real reaction to the property. It always ends the same way, handing off to the deal:

... This week's deal is ...

3

Body

Concrete numbers and features. One idea per line. And the language matches the price tier: an entry-level condo and a luxury listing never sound the same. Entry talks payment and getting in. Luxury talks light, materials, and views, never "bargain."

4

One CTA

A single low-friction reply trigger: "reply and I'll send the details and photos." Never "book a tour." The ask is small on purpose, so the reply is easy.

5

Sign-off

Just your name. No title block, no motivational quote, no "warm regards."

I have a rule: if I would not send it to a friend, it does not make this email. This one made the cut. This week's deal is a 3-bed mountain cabin on a third of an acre.

Illustrative example. No address, no MLS number, no link. The reader has exactly one way to get more: reply.

The Do's

  • ✓Specifics beat hype. A real number, feature, or place name out-pulls any adjective.
  • ✓One concrete number in every subject. Price, beds, distance, year. Something the reader can picture.
  • ✓Write like one person to one person. First person, to a single reader, in your own voice.
  • ✓A different structure every week. Never the same template twice. The framework stays, the shape changes.
  • ✓Frame off-market deals as "off-market." Then hold back the details and let the reply do the selling.
  • ✓Send every single week. Consistency is the strategy. The list you nurture for a year is the one that produces.

The Don'ts

  • ✗Never put the address, MLS number, or listing link in the email. It kills the reply loop. The conversation is the product.
  • ✗No "Hey" openers. Start with the hook, not filler.
  • ✗No hype words. No "dream home," no bare "must see," no "priced to sell" without a concrete reason.
  • ✗No spam symbols. No !!!, no $$$, no ALL CAPS shouting.
  • ✗No fake deadlines. A real "before Thursday" works. Urgency that never expires trains people to ignore you.
  • ✗Never ask for a showing or tour in the first touch. The first ask is information. The tour is the earned next step.
  • ✗Don't skip weeks. One missed week costs more than one mediocre send.
